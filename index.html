Big Data: Connecting the Globe

Looking at the reach of the largest data centers in the United States

Data centers in the US are highly-specialized buildings equipped with power and cooling infrastructure that house computer servers and network equipment. The increasing demand for data storage and consumption is fueling a rapid expansion of data centers in the United States. These data centers are strategically located in regions that offer abundant electricity to meet their high power requirements, ample water for cooling purposes, access to fiber connectivity, affordable real estate, tax incentives, and are situated away from areas prone to natural disasters. The primary locations for data centers in the United States include Northern Virginia, Dallas, Northern California (Silicon Valley), Phoenix, Chicago, Atlanta, Portland (including Hillsboro, Oregon), New York & New Jersey, Seattle (including Quincy, Washington), and Los Angeles.

Instructions For Interactive Map Use:

- The US Data Centers, major rivers, and data travel layers can be toggled by clicking on the buttons in the top left portion of the map.

- The map can be moved top to bottom and left to right by holding the left mouse button while moving the mouse.

- Zoom in or turn off the Data Travel layer to get beyond "the web" of data and inspect the data centers. Clicking on a data center will visualize the continents served by each of the US data centers. Zoom out with the Data Travel layer off to explore the globe uninhibited. Clicking anywhere off a data center will reset the map.

- Zoom, north bearing, and location controls are located top-right. Clicking the north bearing control upon map loading will put map in 2d view.

- Informative pop-ups can be viewed by clicking on summit markers,route lines, or boundary polygons.
